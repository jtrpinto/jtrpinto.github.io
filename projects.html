Curricular Projects

2016

Urinary Bladder CAD System for 3D Reconstruction from CT Scans

Brief description:
Development and evaluation of an algorithm for urinary bladder segmentation in medical images, from contrastless CT studies of patients suffering from bladder wall tumours. These tumours require versatile methods of segmentation, able to adapt to the irregularities on the images obtained, that create adversities to the segmentation process.

Course: Laboratory Projects.
Authors: J.R.Pinto.
Supervisors: Prof. Dr. João Manuel R.S. Tavares.
Keywords: Bladder cancer, CT imaging, image analysis, segmentation, 3D reconstruction.


2016

Senior Data Science: Safe Aging with SPHERE (Challenge)

Brief description:
Open challenge on the use of machine learning methods for the prediction of ambulation activities through multi-sensor data.

Course: Computational Learning.
Authors: J.R.Pinto; P.Rodrigues.
Supervisors: Prof. Dr. Jaime dos Santos Cardoso.
Keywords: Multi-sensor data, machine learning, activity recognition.


2015

Bone Texture Characterization in Radiographs for Osteoporosis Diagnosis

Brief description:
Based on a challenge of the University of Orleans for IEEE ISBI, consisted on the development of an algorithm for automatic extraction of texture features of radiographs of femural bone with and without osteoporosis. Use of the extracted features to build classifiers capable of performing accurate diagnosis of osteoporosis in unknown radiographs.

Course: Computer Aided Diagnosis.
Authors: J.R.Pinto; M.Sousa; S.Oliveira; V.Sá.
Keywords: Osteoporosis, diagnosis, radiograph, texture, feature, machine learning.


2015

vSense: App for Acquisition of Driving Data

Brief description:
Development of an Android application that uses the GPS, an external Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU), the smartphone camera, and the GoogleMaps API to acquire data like location coordinates, speed and acceleration and record to a database, along with videos of moments of sudden braking (possible accidents), and presenting the information, in intuitive and useful ways, to the user.

Course: Mobile Computing in Biomedical Engineering.
Authors: J.R.Pinto; V.Sá.
Keywords: Android, driving, coordinates, speed, acceleration, video, data recording.


2015

Health Clinic Information System: Database and Website

Brief description:
Development of a SQL database to store useful information on the clinic's activities: stock and sold products, staff and their shifts, customers' information, among others. Implementation of an intuitive website to analyse and manage the information on the database.

Course: Information Systems Engineering.
Authors: J.R.Pinto; V.Sá.
Keywords: Health clinic, database, SQL, website, management.


2015

Segmentation and Identification of Pills in Smartphone Images

Brief description:
System for automatic segmentation of pills on smartphone images with variable distances, perspectives, qualities, and positions of the pill. Identification of the segmented pill by extraction of relevant features and comparison to known pills.

Course: Biomedical Image Analysis.
Authors: J.R.Pinto; M.Sousa; S.Oliveira; V.Sá.
Keywords: Pills, images, segmentation, features, identification.


2015

Photoplethysmograph for Heart Rate and Arterial Pressure Measurement

Brief description:
Development of a photoplethysmography (PPG) circuit for for non-invasive measurement of heart rate, by light, at the finger tip, and accurate determination of the systolic and diastolic heart pressure values through the obtained signal. Implementation of adequate analogic and digital signal processing, and presentation of the values in real-time on a graphic user interface.

Course: Biomedical Instrumentation.
Authors: B.Cardoso; J.R.Pinto.
Keywords: PPG, LED, LDR, finger, heart rate, arterial pressure.


2015

PulmoJacket: Respiratory Sounds Acquisition System

Brief description:
Initial development of a wearable system of strategically placed sensors for acquisition, processing and analysis of respiratory sounds on the torso, in order to improve the diagnosis of respiratory infections and diseases through the detection of crackles and wheezes.

Course: Integrated Laboratory in Biomedical Engineering.
Authors: A.Leal; J.R.Pinto.
Keywords: Respiratory sound acquisition, crackles, wheezes, diagnosis, signal processing.


2014

VitalSecurity Biometrics: Personal Identification by ECG

Brief description:
Development of an algorithm for identification of registered individuals based solely on a the comparison of short ECG signals obtained upon registration and upon identification. Implementation of signal processing techniques in order to improve identification results and construction of a friendly graphic user interface.

Course: Physiological Signals Processing.
Authors: A.Leal; J.R.Pinto; P.Rodrigues.
Keywords: Biometrics, ECG, Signal Processing, Identification.


2014

C/C++ Chess Game

Brief description:
Development of an object oriented algorithm in C++ for a two-player chess game, with possibility of automatic illegal moves detection and prevention.

Course: Data Structures and Algorithms.
Authors: A.Leal; J.R.Pinto.
Keywords: C, C++, chess, object-oriented programming, algorithms.


2014

Luminosity Sensor System

Brief description:
Development of a circuit for the acquisition of a signal for environment luminosity levels. Presentation of the signal to the user through an interface offering control over the activation of some light and sound actuators in response to adjustable levels of luminosity.

Course: Sensors, Actuators and Control.
Authors: A.Leal; J.R.Pinto; V.Sá.
Keywords: Luminosity, sensors, circuit, signal acquisition, control.
